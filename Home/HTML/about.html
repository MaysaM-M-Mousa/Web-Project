About Us

From its founding, the La Terra Santa hotel has set high standards in all its hotels, and has been the hotel industry’s standard bearer, through its work procedures and also due to the professional training of all employees.

We believes that its businesses should be managed with integrity, honesty, and adherence to its vision of:

“Providing guests with service above expectations and nurturing employees” - the values to which Dan hotels is uncompromisingly committed. These values are of vital importance in gaining the trust of guests, customers, company employees, business partners, suppliers and shareholders. These binding values are key factors in the Group’s past, present and future success.

The chain has been and continuous to be managed by members of the Federmann family to this day, in order to ensure that the name Terra remains synonymous with excellence in the field of hospitality. Over the years, several of the chain's hotels have won awards, reflecting the endeavors, planning, and attention-to-detail standards to which our staff members are held in order to assure that all Dan guests enjoy “an experience beyond their expectations”

The "La Terra Santa" hotel was opened next to senior "Kaete Dan". The hotel included 120 modern design rooms. Each room had wall-to-wall carpeting, colored bathrooms, telephone and radio. .

1947

The minister of Economy and Industry, Pinchas Sapir persuaded Yekutiel Federmann to purchase the unsuccessful "Accadia" hotel. .

1953

The construction of the "Dan Carmel" hotel was completed. The official grand opening was held in 1963. The exclusive 14 floors hotel included 220 rooms.

1957

The "La Terra Santa" chain purchased from the Baron Edmond de Rothschild the "Golf" hotel in Caesarea. In 1972 the renovated hotel was reopened under the name "Dan Caesarea" hotel. .
